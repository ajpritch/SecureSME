Creating Strong Passwords

What makes password secure and how to make a strong password.


How passwords work

Passwords are one of many forms of authentication and is considered one of the simplest ways to secure information. They work with many cryptographic algorithms in various ways, but they all have one common vulnerability: Time.

Given enough time, all passwords will be eventually be cracked. This is due to the way the cryptographic algorithms work since all they do is simply increase the time required to decrypt as password entropy raises.

To have secure passwords, we must create high entropy. Here are some examples of entropy calculated using KeePassXC and the time required to crack at our current computational capability (not withstanding quantum computer advancements):

  • The letter "a" has 2.00 bits of entropy and can be cracked instantly
  • The word "apple" has 8.32 bits of entropy and can be cracked in about 1 second
  • The code "12345" has 2.58 bits of entropy and can be cracked in about 1 second
  • The passphrase "Venue-Gothic" has 25.85 bits of entropy and can be cracked in about 1 day
  • The passcode "RE9t^aY" has 37.80 bits of entropy and can be cracked in about 1 day
  • The passcode "LzA7NvU3CWth" has 63.97 bits of entropy and can be cracked in about 3 years
  • Your typically recommended 8 character password with numbers, upper and lowercase letters, and special characters "Nc.x90-X" has 45.35 bits of entropy and will lightly take 2 days to crack.
  • The same password as above but with 12 characters "o95_/upw-%JJ" will take about 4 hundred thousand years to crack.
  • The passwords that can be handled by password managers will provide the most time:
  • 72 character passphrase "Decorated-Luckless-Gleaming-Heave-Natural-Unethical-Womb-Backside-Wasabi" has 116.32 bits of entropy and will lightly be cracked in 2 hundred sestrigintillion years
  • The 72 character password "r#&rFXH2Fy%V#i5YU2o#iK7FT$ye#M$ZQ@csc$Z&93$E^Qmw@3tbFzNrc%XD~veTVcDTSN9`" has 435.87 bits of entropy and will lightly be cracked in 41 quadragintillion years (thats 41 followed by 123 zeros)

Entropy is therefore a reasonably good way to measure how much time each password will provide. Even the passwords with 100s of bits of entropy will eventually be cracked.

The real question is how much time do you need? Since the only thing that longer and "more secure" passwords will do is buy you more time.

You can check how long it will lightly take to crack your password using this password testing tool by Bitwarden that calculates locally on your computer.


Password Security

Your circumstances and the security required will determine the required password entropy.

At a minimum, we would recommend using a randomly generated passcode that is a minimum of 10 characters with upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Doing this in a password manager like KeePassXC is easy but bear in mind if you need to type this password a phone for example, then using extended ASCII characters is not always possible. If your using the password frequently and cant autofill with a password manager like KeePassXC then usign a long passphrase might be better, a long passphrase with at least 10 words and a unique separating character like a hyphen or colon etc.. will still work well.

For more information, view the publication by the US governments National Institute of Standards and Technology here. This was published in 2024 and appendix A pertains to password strength.
